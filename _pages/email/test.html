Foobar

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The terms foobar (/ˈfuːbɑr/), fubar, or foo, bar, baz and qux (alternatively, quux) and sometimes norf are sometimes used as placeholder names (also referred to as metasyntactic variables) in computer programming or computer-related documentation.
